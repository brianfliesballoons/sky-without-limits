Paralysis in the United States

An estimated 5.4 million Americans — approximately 1 in 50 — live with some form of paralysis, a figure nearly 40% higher than previous estimates.

Individuals with physical disabilities participate less frequently in recreational activity due to barriers including accessibility, transportation, finances, and limited awareness.

Diaz et al. (2019), Sports Medicine and Arthroscopy Review

A 2023 meta-analysis confirmed positive trends in both physical and mental quality of life outcomes for people with physical disabilities who participate in adaptive sports.

Evidence Base Peer-Reviewed

Why adaptive recreation matters

Peer-reviewed research consistently demonstrates measurable quality of life improvements.

Physical
Improved physical health and functional capacity
Mental
Better mood, reduced depression and anxiety
Social
Increased connectedness and community reintegration
Self
Higher self-esteem, confidence, and life satisfaction

"Regular recreational and sports activity participation has shown to have a positive association with improvements in quality of life, life satisfaction, community reintegration, mood, and employment in those with disabilities."

— Diaz et al. (2019), Sports Medicine and Arthroscopy Review, p. 79

Projected Outcomes Program Targets

What we will measure

A structured evaluation framework using validated instruments to capture immediate and sustained impact.

The outcome targets below are program projections set by Reach for the Stars Foundation — not externally sourced statistics. They represent the benchmarks we aim to meet during the grant period. Survey instruments adapted from the Satisfaction with Life Scale (Diener et al., 1985) and the Positive and Negative Affect Schedule (Watson et al., 1988), both widely validated in disability research.

Outcome Targets (at maximum funding)

Participant satisfaction (recommend to others)90%+
Program completion rate90%+
Report increased sense of freedom70%+
Follow-up survey response rate70%+

Evaluation Methodology

Pre-Flight Survey

Baseline quality of life, confidence, and social connectedness using SWLS and PANAS-adapted scales.

Post-Flight Survey

Immediate impact assessment — same instruments plus satisfaction and recommendation measures.

30-Day Follow-Up

Sustained impact via email and phone. Multiple contact methods and reminders to ensure 70%+ response.

90-Day Follow-Up

Long-term impact. Qualitative data collection — testimonials, photos, and video reflections.

Program Impact Report

Comprehensive analysis shared with the Reeve Foundation — quantitative results, participant stories, and lessons learned.
